## Behavioral Finance
**Lecture 6**

- arbitrage opp. - making money without any risk
- <mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;">Utility theory</mark> - how ppl make decisions when in risk
	- maximize expected utility
	- utility - how good your are off, better off or not? satisfied - benefits
	- problem - not all will be equally off (dependent on the person)
- Risk-aversion
	- risk aversion - take the safe route, stable outcome
		- want premium when taking risk
	- neutral - indifferent
	- risk seeking - take the gamble
- <mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;">Prospect Theory: </mark>Evaluates outcomes as gains or losses relative to a reference point; preferences shift depending on the framing
	  ![[Pasted image 20260104212213.png]]
	- individuals can be both risk-averse and risk-taking based on the framing (on the basis of either loses (risk-seeking) or gains/profits (risk-averse))
	- overweighting low probabilities (like ppl still playing the lottery); underweighting large probs => <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">People do not perceive or apply objective probabilities correctly.</mark>
- Behavioural Biases:
	- Over-confidence
		- overplacement - your skills are superior to others
		- overprecision - ppl are too confident in their prediction precision
	- <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">Disposition:</mark> Ppl realize gains too quickly and hold-onto losing stocks for too long (here, the reference point is the purchase price)
	- <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">Extrapolation</mark> - Investors project recent trends too far into the future, expecting past patterns to persist indefinitely
	  eg., during bull market ppl expect gains to go up forever, but in bear market ppl are not willing to invest at all
	- <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">Herding</mark> - opposite of overconfidence; following other ppl who you believe are better investors
		- thinking this group of investors/investor have some information
		- don't want to go against the crowd based on the sentiment on the stock
		- which brings even more ppl into the stock which pushes prices more (momentum effect) = bubbly
	- Usually overconfidence comes first followed by herding
	- Unified Theory - 
		- setup: investors => Newswatchers (managers, tracking fundamentals, insiders, etc.) & Momentum Traders (herders, etc. only looking at prices - is it going up or down and think this ought to continue)
		- even for those who get info. - info. does not diffuse instantaneously, so even Newswatchers start to invest at different point in time which drives up prices gradually and the gains start to go down - underreacting to the fundamentals
		- momentum traders - helps the fundamental adjustment as the Newswatchers have been underreacting, but at some point this becomes harmful, the Newswatchers know what the fundamentals show the prices should have gone by, and once the prices go above this price - so they start to short-stocks or sell stocks, and then the Momentum Traders follow this shorting again [slide 25]
			- there is empirical evidence so to support this as momentum strategies focusing on 6-12 months are profitable and small-stock momentum trading is down to be profitable, but then evidence for Reversal as well (reversal strong is momentum was strong)
		- Event - day 0
		- [slide 29] - mispricing
		- Risk based good?
	- investor sentiment - thing that is really driving the participation in the market isn't just the prices, but sentiment, if sentiment is low, ppl don't want to invest in the speculative stocks; much higher mispricing during the time of very high or bad sentiments = systematic issues with under/over valuation
		- when sentiment high = more IPOs, more venture capital going in, more M&As
