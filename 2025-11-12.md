- ## <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">Fama-French 3-factor model</mark>: Size and value effects are systematic risk factors
	- The core "big idea" is that **size and value effects are systematic risk factors**. Fama and French observed that small-cap companies and stocks with high book-to-market ratios (value stocks) consistently outperformed the broader market. They argued that this outperformance isn't just "luck" but a **reward** for taking on specific risks that the standard CAPM beta doesn't capture.
	- ![[Pasted image 20260104180759.png]]
	  SMBt​ (Small Minus Big): The average return of small-cap stock portfolios minus the average return of large-cap portfolios.
	  HMLt​ (High Minus Low): The average return of "value" portfolios (high book-to-market) minus the average return of "growth" portfolios (low book-to-market).
	- Profitability & Investment added in 2015 = 5-factor model now
